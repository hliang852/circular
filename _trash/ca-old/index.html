What companies do with capital
tells you more than what they say.

Circular tracks every repurchase mandate, monthly buyback filing, equity issuance, and AGM date for HKEX-listed companies — and surfaces the signals that matter to equity analysts.

🔄
Share Repurchases
Monthly Return filings give you volume, price range, and cumulative progress against the AGM-approved mandate. We surface the VWAP floor — where the company itself has been buying — as a price support level.
📋
Mandate Tracking
Every mandate is capped at 10% of issued capital by Listing Rule 10.06. Circular shows headroom remaining, float-adjusted impact, and a probability score for renewal at the next AGM.
⚡
Equity Issuances
Placings, convertible bonds, and rights issues directly offset buybacks. We show gross buybacks alongside net capital return so you see the true picture.
🤝
DI Cross-reference
When a company buys back shares AND a substantial shareholder is simultaneously accumulating, both buyers are competing for the same shrinking float. That compound signal is surfaced on every stock page.
What the signals mean
● Strong
The company is buying back shares below its own 12-month VWAP, AND a substantial shareholder is accumulating via DI filing. Both buyers are active at current prices.
● Moderate
The company is buying below its own VWAP with a consistency score of 4+, but no concurrent DI accumulation is detected.
● Mandate Renewer
Company has an AGM within 90 days with >65% probability of mandate renewal. Uninterrupted programme support into the next fiscal year.

Repurchase Mandate
What is the repurchase mandate?

Under HK Listing Rule 10.06, companies need shareholder approval at each AGM. The mandate caps total repurchases until the next AGM — standard limit is 10% of issued shares.
